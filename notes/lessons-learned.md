# Lessons Learned

## 1. Plan Before You Build
I need to take time upfront to map out what I'm building before jumping in. Having a clear diagram and rough outline of the steps before starting would have saved significant troubleshooting time and made the project flow much smoother.

## 2. Know Your Ports Before You Need Them
Understanding which ports are required for each service ahead of time would have prevented several of the connectivity issues I ran into. Before starting a project like this, it's worth mapping out the services being used and the ports they depend on.

## 3. Budget for the Unexpected
If I think something will take an hour, I should plan for two. Projects like this rarely go exactly as expected, and that's okay — building in contingency time removes the pressure of being caught off guard when things take longer than anticipated.

## 4. Enjoy the Problem Solving
Projects are never going to be perfectly smooth, and that's the point. Every unexpected issue is a learning opportunity. Finding the enjoyment in troubleshooting and figuring things out is what makes this kind of work valuable.
